Adaptive monitoring with artificial intelligence

The topic of the thesis is focused on research of adaptive network monitoring and workload management with AI technology such as machine learning, deep neural networks and/or nature-inspired optimization. Various methods will work adaptively and precisely within predefined time constraints. The topic is also focused on dynamic and growing data, which requires paying attention only to certain aspects. The work is closely related to scalable and/or stream processing in-situ. PhD students choose one of the two domains: cyber-security or workload management.

Collaborative learning with sensitive data protection

Many modern-world problems, which we want to solve with the help of AI, require direct access to sensitive information such as personal, medical, business or security information. In many cases, getting access to such information is nearly impossible due to the protection requirements. From this point, several questions are raised, such as how to avoid sensitive information disclosure in the data science process or moreover, how can we model sensitive data without direct access to them. The topic of the work is focused on collaborative (federated) learning, where it is necessary to ensure knowledge sharing between partners, while ensuring the protection of sensitive data at the same time.

Sequence modelling with context-aware attention

The most exciting advances in data science using intelligent methods are thanks to dynamic and growing amounts of data, which also raise new challenges. Unless the data is not labelled, it remains essentially useless for many applications relying on supervised learning. Since unlabeled instances are easy to obtain, winning the AI race comes through a flexible and scalable way of obtaining quality training datasets automatically. Another challenge related to dynamic and growing data is to pay attention on certain aspects while devoting the rest to less weight. The thesis will focus on active learning with attention mechanisms. It relates to a context-aware approach for sequence to sequence modeling.
