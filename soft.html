Soft computing

Soft computing is an emerging approach to computing which parallel the remarkable ability of human mind to reason and learn in the environment of uncertainly and imprecision. (Lotfi A. Zadeh, 1994)

Applied Soft Computing (ASC, http://www.softcomputing.org, 2019) constructs computationally intelligent methods by combining emerging problem solving technologies such as fuzzy logic, probabilistic computing and reasoning, artificial neural networks, machine learning and evolutionary computation to solve complex and real world problems. Various methods used in soft computing are neither independent of nor compete with one another, but rather, they work in a cooperative and complementary way. It aims to tolerance of imprecision, uncertainty, partial truth and approximation to achieve effectiveness and low cost solutions.

When today data has dynamic and scalable potencial with Volume, Velocity, Variety and Veracity characteristics, the proper combination of intelligent soft computing methods with scalable data processing, and high-performance backgrounds is practical to face challenges in many areas. All of these advanced technologies mentioned above do not have to be always coupled together, but the alliance is essential for complex solutions.
